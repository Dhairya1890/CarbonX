Humans emit approximately 106 million metric tons (106.2 billion kg) of CO₂ per day globally.

That's equivalent to

  • Emissions from ~21 billion liters of gasoline burned daily
  • The weight of ~17 Great Pyramids of Giza in CO₂ gas per day
  • 106 million tons could melt 318 million m² of Arctic ice — larger than New York City (~780 km²) every day

Some Hard - Hitting Facts

Earth Is on Track for 1.5°C Rise by Early 2030s

According to the IPCC, we're likely to exceed 1.5°C warming between 2030–2035 if drastic cuts aren't made.

2023 Was the Hottest Year Ever Recorded

Global average temperature in 2023 exceeded 1.48°C above pre-industrial levels.

Arctic Sea Ice Is Vanishing

The Arctic could see ice-free summers as early as the 2030s.

Sea Levels Are Rising Faster Than Ever

sea levels are rising at 4.5 mm/year, double the rate in the 20th century.

Biodiversity Loss Is at Mass Extinction Scale

Over 1 million species are at risk of extinction, many within decades.

Call to Action - Step by Step

Climate change is mainly caused by humans burning fossil fuels, cutting forests, and farming on a massive scale. This releases gases that trap heat and overheat our planet. Melting ice, rising seas, and wild weather are the result. Learning about this helps everyone see why we must act—awareness is the spark!
You can’t fix what you don’t measure. From global emissions to your personal carbon footprint, tracking emissions tells us where the biggest problems are. Think of it as checking your health before starting treatment. Measure to manage!
Once we know where emissions come from, we start cutting them. Use clean energy (solar, wind), switch to public transport or electric vehicles, waste less, and eat sustainably. Everyone—governments, companies, and individuals—has a part to play. Less carbon, more future!
Time to get creative! Smart solutions like carbon capture, plant-based meat, or green cities help us go beyond just reducing emissions. Innovation helps us adapt and build a cooler, cleaner world. Let ideas power the planet!
